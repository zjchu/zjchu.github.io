SiaTalker: Siamese Emotion Injection for Coarse-to-fine Speech-driven 3D Facial Animation

Yilin Lan†, Zhaojie Chu†, Jianxiu Jin, Xiangmin Xu, Xiaofen Xing*
†Equal Contribution, *Corresponding author.
Paper arXiv Video Code

SiaTalker can synthesize vivid 3D facial animations (mesh sequences) given audio snippets.

Abstract

During communication, people inadvertently express genuine emotions through facial animations, with emotion constantly fluctuating throughout the conversation. Existing speech-driven facial animation works predominantly rely on sentence-level coarse-grained emotion labels to impart emotional expression information, thereby neglecting the dynamic evolution of emotional expression.

How to make 3D talking heads articulate fluently while adeptly expressing mood fluctuations is the problem addressed by our work. We propose a framework, named SiaTalker, which exquisitely refines emotions of facial animations from coarse-grained levels to dual-perspective interactive fine-grained levels, resulting in highly coordinated and fluid emotional expressions. Specifically, we propose the coarse-to-fine dual granularity fusion decoding framework to connect global and local emotional aspects, allowing nuanced shifts within a given emotional theme. The pseudo-siamese cross-perspective submodule is embedded to interact with siamese fine-grained emotions across perspectives, further capturing subtle emotional fluctuations and amplifying the authenticity of facial movements. Furthermore, to comprehensively evaluate our work, the CREMA-D dataset is reconstructed into a 3D emotional talking face dataset with the largest number of subjects, serving as a valuable experimental supplement.

Extensive experiments and user studies demonstrate that our approach outperforms state-of-the-art methods and exhibits superior emotional agility and expressiveness in facial movements.

Method

Overview of the proposed SiaTalker. The coarse-to-fine dual granularity fusion decoding framework is designed to generate a series of 3D facial animations with richer dynamic emotions from speech. The design of the audio encoder follows wavLM. In the coarse-grained stage, the global emotional type of the sequence and speech features are jointly decoded to establish the specific emotional themes of facial movements. In the fine-grained stage, a pseudo-siamese network synchronously captures the temporal variations of facial emotion expression from distinct perspectives: pitch and energy. The cross-perspective interaction mechanism is embedded to facilitate complementary interaction between emotional fluctuations of siamese branches.
